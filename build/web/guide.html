Underweight Guide

Being underweight means that your weight is below the healthy weight range, if you are underweight you might find yourself get sick often, or feel very tired. That may be because you are not getting the right nutrients you need from your diet. You may also find your hair gets thinner or falls out, your skin gets very dry and your teeth are affected. Being underweight can pose several health risks that may include:

  • Weakened immune system
  • Susceptibility to illness and infections
  • Nutrient deficiencies
  • Reduced energy levels
  • Osteoporosis (Bone Weakness)

                      Overweight Guide

                      Being overweight means that your weight is above the healthy weight range, if left untreated, it presents serious health risks and can result in a number of complications. Overweight people frequently have excess body fat, which puts stress on important organs and raises the possibility of chronic illnesses.Being overweight can pose several health risks that may include:

                      • Type 2 diabetes
                      • Heart disease
                      • High blood pressure
                      • Strokes
                      • Joint problems

                      Moreover, being overweight can impact overall quality of life, leading to:
                      • Reduced mobility
                      • Low self-esteem,
                      • Mental health issues such as depression and anxiety

                      Recognizing the symptoms of overweight, such as difficulty in performing physical activities, fatigue, shortness of breath, and chronic joint pain, is crucial for early intervention and effective management of weight-related health concerns.

                                Obesity Guide

                                An excessive build-up of body fat is the hallmark of Obesity, its a quite complex health condition that can have significant effects on one's physical and mental health. It can lower the overall standard of life and greatly raise the risk of developing a number of serious health complications.
                                Some of the key risks associated with obesity includes the likelihood of developing:

                                • Type 2 diabetes
                                • Cardiovascular diseases
                                • Hypertension
                                • Cancer
                                • Respiratory issues
                                • Musculoskeletal disorders
                                • Gastrointestinal Disorders
                                • Sleep Disorders
                                • Skin Conditions
                                • Psychological Effects
                                • Poor Wound Healing
                                The list goes on and on and its clear that obesity is absolutely horrific, the journey to a healthy body from here will be nothing but hardwork and patience.
